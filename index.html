Jessica Kong

I'm currently a postdoctoral researcher in the Pfaendnter Research Group, where I work on applied natural language processing.

I completed my PhD in Physical Chemistry at the University of Washington. My research broadly utilized ML/NLP tools on multimodal data. I have worked on developing a machine framework for evaluating content-based information retrieval of scientific text; developing next generation solar cell materials via nanoscale characterization; elucidating relationships between chemical structure and electrical function in polymeric materials; and developing a fundamental understanding of how niche atomic force microscopy methods work.

Before joining the University of Washington, I completed my undergraduate studies in chemistry at Middlebury College where I studied the thermal decomposition of thiols with AJ Vasiliou. Before Middlebury, I went to Oakland High.

My experience as a first-generation, low-income college student drives my work to make the spaces I'm in more equitable, diverse, and inclusive. I founded Middlebury College Access Mentors, served as a mentor within the UW Dream Project, and was on the board of the UW First-Generation Graduate Students. I also read extensively on this topic to integrate research into the way I engage. I particularly like this article and this book.

Other

I wrote a tutorial for atomic force microscopists (assuming little coding knowledge) to register images which enables true multimodal analysis.

I've also contributed code to a package for analyzing nanoscale imaging data. My efforts include a script to translate proprietary file format to HDF5 and functionality to read and process data from a niche atomic force microscopy technique (~all the other commits).

I've written and pitched proposals to get the instrument that enabled a few of my published works and funding from both the NSF and internal UW organizations to fund a large portion of my PhD.
