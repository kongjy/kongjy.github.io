Jessica Kong

I'm currently a postdoctoral researcher in the Pfaendnter Research Group , where I work on applied natural language processing. My work is focused on how the scientific community can adapt and apply NLP tools to leverage the vast amounts of information that already exists in scientific documents. I completed my PhD in Chemistry at the University of Washington during which I broadly utilized ML/NLP tools on multimodal data. Before joining the University of Washington, I completed my undergraduate studies in chemistry at Middlebury College where I studied the thermal decomposition of thiols with AJ Vasiliou. Before Middlebury, I went to Oakland High.

My experiences as a first-generation, low-income college student drives my work to make the spaces I'm in more equitable, diverse, and inclusive. I founded Middlebury College Access Mentors, served as a mentor within the UW Dream Project, and on the board of the First-Generation Graduate Students.
