Jessica Kong

Hello, welcome! I was most recently a Senior Solutions Engineer at Databricks, where I partnered with customers to build technical solutions to realize business value from their use of Databricks. My work spanned three areas: prototyping on-platform and via APIs/SDKs to unlock workloads; educating while removing technical blockers to enable customers; and working closely with product, engineering, and support to deliver scalable solutions.

Previously, I was an applied scientist in the Artificial Intelligence and Data Analytics Division at Pacific Northwest National Laboratory, where I guided and drove projects in natural language processing and generative AI. I obtained my PhD in Physical Chemistry at the University of Washington while under the supervision of Jim Pfaendtner and Dave Beck. There, I transitioned from using instruments to characterize materials to technical solution development within chemistry, which enabled me to effectively communicate and collaborate across a wide range of scientific and engineering disciplines.

Before joining the University of Washington, I completed my undergraduate studies in chemistry at Middlebury College where I built an instrument to study the thermal decomposition of thiols with AJ Vasiliou.

In essence, I'm passionate about constructing solutions, whether they're tangible or digital, and I'm energized by the dynamic of working in collaborative teams.
